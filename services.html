Important Client Information

Before Booking a Tiling Job

Site Access

The client must provide safe access to the work area, available water and electricity, and clear space for tools, materials and movement.

Materials

If tiles or materials are supplied by the client, builder or third party, Top 10 Tiling is not responsible for defective, damaged, warped, delayed or unsuitable materials.

Variations

Changes to tile size, design, pattern, layout, scope or site instructions may affect price, labour and completion time. Variations should be confirmed in writing.

Payments

Payments must be made according to the agreed quote or contract. Late payments may result in delays, suspension of works or recovery action.

Standards & Compliance

Industry Standards & Legal Notes

Top 10 Tiling aims to complete works in line with applicable Australian construction standards and NSW requirements where relevant.

Relevant legislation and standards may include the Home Building Act 1989 (NSW), Australian Consumer Law, Work Health and Safety Act 2011 (NSW), Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 1999 (NSW), National Construction Code, AS 3958.1 for ceramic tile installation and AS 3740 for waterproofing domestic wet areas.

This website provides general business information only and does not replace a written quote, written contract or legal advice.
