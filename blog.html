Helpful Tiling Guides

Learn what to consider before starting bathroom, floor, outdoor, kitchen and waterproofing projects.


Bathroom Tiling

What to Know Before Starting Bathroom Tiling

Bathroom tiling needs proper planning, waterproofing, tile layout, grout selection, drainage consideration and clear scope of work before starting.

  • Confirm tile size and tile type
  • Check waterproofing requirements
  • Plan shower areas, niches and floor wastes
  • Confirm grout colour and silicone finish

Floor Tiling

How to Prepare for Floor Tiling

Good floor tiling starts with a suitable surface, correct levelling, tile layout planning and access to the work area.

  • Clear the work area before the job
  • Confirm surface condition
  • Prepare access for materials and tools
  • Allow curing time before heavy use

Kitchen Splashbacks

Choosing the Right Kitchen Splashback

Kitchen splashbacks should match the kitchen style, bench colour, lighting, cabinet finish and cleaning needs.

  • Choose easy-to-clean tiles
  • Consider grout colour carefully
  • Match tile finish with cabinet design
  • Plan power points and edges

Outdoor Tiling

Important Tips for Outdoor Tiling

Outdoor tiling requires suitable tiles, correct fall, drainage planning, weather consideration and proper preparation.

  • Use suitable outdoor tiles
  • Consider slip resistance
  • Plan water drainage
  • Check expansion and movement joints

Large Format Tiles

Why Large Format Tiles Need Specialist Handling

Large format tiles can create a luxury modern look, but they require careful handling, cutting, levelling and installation.

  • Tiles can be heavier and harder to handle
  • Surfaces must be properly prepared
  • Accurate levelling is important
  • Cutting and installation require care

Premium Finishes

Stone & Premium Tile Finishes

Stone and premium finishes can improve the look of bathrooms, kitchens, feature walls and commercial areas.

  • Natural variation is normal in stone
  • Sealing may be required for some products
  • Edges and trims should be planned early
  • Maintenance requirements should be considered

Renovation Planning

Before You Start a Tiling Project

A clear plan helps reduce delays, cost changes and misunderstandings during a tiling project.

Information to Prepare

  • Project address or suburb
  • Photos of the work area
  • Approximate measurements
  • Tile size and tile type
  • Preferred start date
  • Any design or layout ideas

Questions to Ask

  • Is waterproofing required?
  • Are tiles supplied by client or contractor?
  • Is the surface ready for tiling?
  • Are other trades finished?
  • Is access clear?
  • Are payment stages agreed?

Common Causes of Delay

  • Late tile delivery
  • Wrong materials supplied
  • Change of tile size or design
  • Other trades not finished
  • Restricted site access
  • Delayed payments or approvals

Best Practice

  • Use written quotes
  • Confirm scope before starting
  • Confirm variations in writing
  • Keep project photos
  • Keep invoice and payment records
  • Communicate early about issues

Legal & Standards

Important Building Notes

Tiling projects may be affected by site conditions, material quality, tile type, surface preparation, waterproofing requirements, structural movement, weather and other trades.

Relevant laws and standards may include the Home Building Act 1989 (NSW), Australian Consumer Law, Work Health and Safety Act 2011 (NSW), Building and Construction Industry Security of Payment Act 1999 (NSW), National Construction Code, AS 3958.1 for ceramic tile installation and AS 3740 for waterproofing domestic wet areas.
